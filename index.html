I am currently a Fellow at Insight Data Science New York. Before coming to Insight I was a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Integrative Biology working with Dr. Mathew Leibold at the University of Texas at Austin. I earned my Ph.D. in Ecology, Evolution & Behavior, also at UT Austin, in 2013.

While at Insight, I built a web app to help unpack the opinions of citizens who submitted comments to the FCC on their net-neutrality policy reversal. You can take a closer look here.

My doctoral research has focused on the role of competition in structuring communities of cryptic amphipod species. I’ve also worked on the morphometrics of damselfly wings, the multidimensional nature of ecosystem resilience, developing curriculum for a new biostatistics course at UT Austin, and collaborating on an analysis of the punctuation in Shakespeare’s plays, through my work as a Graduate Fellow in the Division of Statistics & Scientific Computation at UT Austin.

Originally from Montréal, Québec, I also hold a M.Sc. in Biology and a B.Sc. in Biology & History, both from McGill University.
